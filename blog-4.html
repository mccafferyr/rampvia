The Hidden Costs of Buying the Wrong GSE — and How to Avoid Them

By Juan San Martin

Introduction

Mistakes in Ground Support Equipment (GSE) purchasing can be expensive. Poor sourcing decisions quickly lead to hidden costs, operational disruptions, and long-term financial consequences.

Common Hidden Costs

Operational Inefficiency

If GSE doesn't fit your operational needs — wrong tow capacity, incorrect voltage, improper sizing — it can slow down ground handling processes and increase staffing demands.

Maintenance and Repairs

Equipment lacking proper service history often results in frequent repairs and premature replacement costs, making cheap "deals" far more expensive over time.

Reputation and Revenue Risks

Operational delays caused by faulty GSE can damage brand reputation, disrupt flight schedules, and negatively impact revenue streams.
