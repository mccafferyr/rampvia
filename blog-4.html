The Hidden Costs of Buying the Wrong GSE — and How to Avoid Them

By Juan San Martin

Mistakes in Ground Support Equipment (GSE) purchasing can be expensive. When buyers choose the wrong equipment — whether due to rushed decisions, incomplete specifications, or supplier issues — the hidden costs quickly add up.

At Rampvia, we work with aviation professionals every day who have seen the impact of bad sourcing decisions. From increased maintenance expenses to operational downtime, the real cost of buying the wrong GSE goes far beyond the initial purchase price.

The first major hidden cost is operational inefficiency. If the equipment doesn't meet the specific needs of your operation — whether it’s the wrong tow capacity, the wrong voltage for GPUs, or improper sizing for belt loaders — it can cause slowdowns, delays, and higher staffing requirements.

The second hidden cost is maintenance. Equipment that isn't properly vetted or that lacks proper service history can result in expensive repairs or premature replacements. What looks like a good deal upfront can quickly become a major drain on your maintenance budget.

Finally, there’s the reputational cost. Ground operations delays impact not just airline schedules but passenger satisfaction and revenue. Reliable GSE is essential to maintaining high operational standards.

At Rampvia, we help clients avoid these pitfalls by carefully matching equipment to operational needs, ensuring quality standards are met, and working only with trusted, vetted suppliers. Sourcing smarter isn’t just about finding a lower price — it’s about reducing total cost of ownership and improving long-term performance.

If you’re ready to source GSE the right way the first time, partner with Rampvia for smarter aviation solutions.
